Peter Manheim
Drummer, Percussionist, Educator
About

Peter Manheim is a drummer, percussionist, and educator from Chicago, Illinois. He holds a Bachelor of Music degree from the Oberlin Conservatory of Music, where he studied primarily with Billy Hart and Jamey Haddad. While studying at Oberlin, Peter spent two years working as an accompanist (playing percussion) for various modern dance classes that the college offered for students. Teachers he worked for included Elesa Rosasco and Nusha Martynuk.

Peter performs internationally and has had the privilege of performing with such artists as Gary Bartz, Wallace Roney, Leo Blanco and Alon Yavnai. Peter also recently completed a tour of performing and teaching in China with the Terry Hsieh Collective, including a teaching residency at the International School of Beijing (IBS). Peter plays both drum set and auxiliary percussion. His interest and love of Brazilian music lead him to Brazil, from where he recently returned after 6 months of studying, teaching, and performing music in the states of São Paulo, Parana, Rio de Janeiro, Bahia, and Pernambuco. While in São Paulo, Peter taught private drum lessons and jazz improvisation classes at Souza Lima College and Conservatory.

Peter strives to fuse his love and knowledge of the jazz tradition with contemporary and global musical influences.
